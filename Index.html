Professional Summary

Research scientist with a multidisciplinary background in nanomaterials and their applications

PhD in Materials Science and Nanotechnology and a strong multidisciplinary background in the synthesis and surface functionalization of hybrid magnetic–plasmonic nanoparticles for biomedical, sensing, and catalytic applications, my doctoral work focused on designing SERS-active nanostructures and investigating their interactions with microglial cells to elucidate nanoparticle–cell dynamics in brain-relevant models.

I am particularly drawn to research groups working at the interface of nanoscience and applied engineering, especially those focused on developing advanced hybrid inorganic–organic materials, thin-film coatings, and bio-organic nanomaterials. My prior experience in thin-film fabrication for antimicrobial food packaging and proteomic analysis of tissue samples complements this focus and provides me with a broad perspective on the interplay between functional materials and biological environments.

I am eager to contribute my expertise to the development of smart nanomaterials and their translation into real-world biomedical, environmental, or sensing applications.
